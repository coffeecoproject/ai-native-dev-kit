# IntentOS 1.104.1 Known Limitations

## Semantic Enforcement Is Not Business Proof

The semantic checker can reject known responsibility-model drift. It cannot
prove that a business rule, legal fact, tax fact, provider state, or real-world
effect is correct.

## Compatibility Vocabulary Remains

Historical artifacts and compatibility schemas still contain terms such as
`Human Approval`, `owner`, and `reviewer`. Their presence does not make the user
responsible for technical judgment. A current consumer must translate them
through the four current user-input classes.

## Exact Consent Still Applies

IntentOS/Codex may prepare and explain a real-world action, but silence and a
general request are not consent. Concrete production, cost, payment, external
account, real-user communication, and irreversible data effects still require
exact bounded consent or an external authority fact.

## Project-Native Rules Remain Authoritative Inputs

Existing project baselines and governance are reconciled rather than silently
overwritten. Codex chooses the technical route, but still needs evidence,
controlled-apply readiness, rollback, and the applicable project authority
before writing governed assets.

## Language Detection Is Bounded

The checker covers registered current guidance and known English/Chinese drift
patterns. Novel phrasing can still require future calibration. The independent
review-context tests and generated-project checks exist to make such gaps
visible without weakening the product contract.
