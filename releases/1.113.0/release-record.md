# IntentOS 1.113.0 Release Record

## Theme

Cross-Domain Consumer And Behavioral Closure Hardening.

## Result

IntentOS 1.113 closes the P0 and P1 root causes recorded by the 1.112
nine-domain audit. Required evidence is now consumed through typed,
fail-closed outcomes; minimum task obligations no longer depend on optional
Business Universe depth; Completion Evidence revalidates current Execution
Assurance; public finish has one final technical evidence authority; effective
guidance covers actual runtime and generated outputs; and controlled apply and
existing-project activation are proved across interruption and a fresh
session.

## Delivered

- one typed strict-consumer outcome contract;
- proportional minimum task obligations for LOW through HIGH work;
- current Execution Assurance consumption by Completion Evidence;
- one fail-closed public completion authority;
- required Change Boundary and Standard Baseline evidence on applicable
  routes;
- expanded Effective Guidance discovery and responsibility enforcement;
- strict public release-source continuity before real-world consent review;
- durable controlled-apply journaling, interruption recovery, and conflict
  blocking;
- short-lived, single-use request authority bound to the original goal, current
  project/revision/source state, canonical plan, and exact reversible local
  action graph;
- behavior-complete existing-project adoption that preserves legacy agent rules,
  creates the canonical Codex entry, and proves the next project-local session;
- generated, updated, installed, and next-session behavior probes;
- P0/P1 closure evidence and a reclassified nineteen-edge matrix.

## Allowed Claims

- The P0 and P1 findings promoted by the 1.112 audit are closed by current
  implementation and adversarial tests.
- The nineteen required consumer edges were reclassified without a known
  P0/P1 fail-open, duplicate final authority, or disconnected P0/P1 consumer.
- Missing applicable evidence cannot satisfy current completion or release
  authority through compatibility-only results.
- Existing-project activation and controlled-apply interruption recovery have
  current behavior tests.
- IntentOS remains a zero-experience solo-user system with one natural-language
  public entry.

## Forbidden Claims

- IntentOS eliminates every possible defect or production risk.
- Passing governance proves business, legal, security, or production
  correctness without relevant evidence.
- Technical readiness authorizes deployment, store submission, migration,
  payment, cost, real-user communication, or another external effect.
- The user must choose internal domains, checkers, task levels, baselines,
  reviewers, or remediation strategy.
- This release authorizes 1.114 repository restructuring without its separate
  plan and migration evidence.

## Boundaries

This release introduces no new public command, user-visible lifecycle,
nine-step workflow, completion state machine, or external execution authority.
The nine domains remain internal audit lenses. Compatibility evidence remains
readable but cannot satisfy current strict authority.

The user supplies real goals, unavailable business or external facts, bounded
product preferences, and consent to one exact prepared real-world effect.
Codex owns technical discovery, planning, implementation, verification,
review, evidence, repair, and readiness recommendations.
