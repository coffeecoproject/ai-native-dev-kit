# IntentOS 1.110.0 Release Record

## Theme

Control Effectiveness.

## Result

IntentOS now distinguishes control registration or project ownership from
claim-scoped, current, proven effectiveness. A strict adoption, plan,
verification, execution, or completion conclusion cannot rely on a gate,
checker, test, policy, or workflow merely because it exists, runs, or reports
PASS.

## Delivered

- one structured Control Effectiveness evidence contract;
- exact project, task, intent, implementation, inventory, and evidence binding;
- semantic, scope, freshness, failure-capability, result-integrity, and safety
  validation;
- bounded direct-process adapter validation without evidence-provided shell
  execution;
- conditional Task Governance and Plan Review routing;
- strict propagation through Verification Plan, Test Evidence, Execution
  Assurance, Completion Evidence, and public finish checks;
- project ownership/effectiveness separation in Existing Rule Reconciliation,
  Governance Convergence, and Adoption Assurance;
- source and generated-project distribution with one unchanged public `work`
  entry.

## Allowed Claims

- IntentOS can distinguish a registered control from current, claim-scoped
  evidence that the control actually enforces its stated behavior.
- Current strict consumers can fail closed when required Control Effectiveness
  evidence is missing, stale, copied, semantically mismatched, or incapable of
  demonstrating a safe representative failure.
- Control Effectiveness can be routed conditionally by task risk and propagated
  through the structured planning, verification, execution, completion, and
  adoption evidence chains.
- Generated generic, Web, iOS, and Android projects receive the same 1.110
  Control Effectiveness contracts and checkers as the source repository.

## Forbidden Claims

- A registered, present, or passing control is effective without exact 1.110
  evidence for the current project, task, intent, implementation, and claim.
- A proven control proves the product, business rules, tests, release, or
  production environment correct outside the bounded claim it evaluates.
- Control Effectiveness authorizes implementation, project writes, adoption
  apply, CI or hook mutation, release, production, or a final completion
  decision.
- Historical reports automatically satisfy current strict 1.110 consumers.

## Boundaries

Control Effectiveness is a read-only internal evidence source. It does not
authorize implementation, project writes, CI or hook changes, adoption apply,
release, production, or final completion. Unified Closure remains the final
task close-out authority. A proven control does not prove product or business
correctness.

## Compatibility

Historical reports remain readable with their original meaning. Current strict
claims require 1.110 evidence. Unrelated tasks do not receive a project-wide
control audit, and stronger project-native rules remain authoritative even when
their enforcement proof still needs technical remediation.

## Evidence Status

The release counts as complete only when the exact 1.110 release candidate
passes focused positive and adversarial Control Effectiveness tests, strict
consumer-chain and adoption tests, cross-version Business Universe and Runtime
Trust regressions, generated-project parity, Manifest and Review Context
authority checks, the full repository verification suite, and the final diff
boundary check.
